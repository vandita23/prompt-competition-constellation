URSA MAJOR

Even the darkest sky contains stars that guide the lost.

The Great Bear

Ursa Major, meaning “The Great Bear,” is one of the most recognizable constellations in the night sky. It has been observed for thousands of years across cultures and civilizations.

Ancient astronomers imagined shapes among the stars to tell stories about the universe. Among them, Ursa Major became a symbol of strength and cosmic presence.

The Big Dipper

Within Ursa Major lies a famous pattern of seven stars known as the Big Dipper. Its shape resembles a ladle or spoon and has guided travelers for centuries.

Two stars in the dipper point toward Polaris, the North Star, which helped explorers navigate across oceans and deserts.

The Big Dipper Reveals Itself

A Symbol of Hope

The Big Dipper reminds us that even small lights can guide us through darkness. For centuries people looked up to the stars when they felt lost.

Just like the constellation leads travelers to the North Star, hope in life often begins with a single point of light.

Look Up

The universe is vast and mysterious, yet it constantly reminds us that darkness is never truly empty. It is filled with countless lights waiting to be seen.

Sometimes all it takes is a few stars to show the way forward.
