URSA MAJOR

Even in the darkest skies, the stars will guide you forward.

The Great Bear

Ursa Major, the Great Bear, has inspired humans for thousands of years. Ancient civilizations saw it as a giant bear wandering the sky, a cosmic guide that never sleeps. It symbolizes something bigger than ourselves — a constant reminder that no matter how dark life feels, guidance exists.

Across cultures, Ursa Major was a compass, a storybook, and a source of hope. Even when lost, people looked up at its stars to find direction.

The Big Dipper

Within Ursa Major lies the Big Dipper — seven bright stars forming a ladle-like shape. Its simplicity made it a universal symbol for navigation. The pointer stars direct travelers to Polaris, the North Star, steadfast in the night sky.

For centuries, sailors, wanderers, and explorers relied on these stars. The Big Dipper taught humanity a lesson: even a small pattern of light can guide you through vast darkness.

The Big Dipper Reveals Itself

Hope in the Darkness

The Big Dipper is more than a constellation. It teaches us resilience. When life feels vast and uncertain, even the smallest glimmer of light can guide us forward. Look at the stars, and remember: there is always a path, always hope, even in the darkest night.
