URSA MAJOR

Even the darkest sky contains stars that guide us forward.

The Great Bear

Ursa Major is one of the most recognizable constellations in the northern sky. For thousands of years humans have looked toward it for meaning, navigation, and hope.

Inside this great constellation lies a smaller pattern called the Big Dipper. Seven stars that together form a shape resembling a ladle in the sky.

The Hidden Compass

Two stars of the Big Dipper always point toward the North Star. Travelers once used this celestial pattern to navigate through the unknown.

Even when lost in darkness, the sky offered a quiet reminder that direction always exists somewhere above.

The Big Dipper

Never Lose Hope

The universe can feel vast and overwhelming, just like life sometimes does. Yet inside that vastness small patterns appear.

Ursa Major reminds us that even a few stars together can guide travelers across oceans and deserts.

In the darkest sky, the smallest light can still show the way.
