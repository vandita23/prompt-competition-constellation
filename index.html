The Great Bear

High above the northern sky stretches one of the most famous constellations in astronomy. Ursa Major has fascinated civilizations for thousands of years.

Within this vast constellation lies a simple pattern of seven stars known as the Big Dipper.

The Hidden Compass

Two stars of the Big Dipper point toward the North Star. Travelers once used this celestial pattern to navigate across oceans and deserts.

Even when the world felt dark and uncertain, the stars above provided direction.
