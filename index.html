URSA MAJOR

A constellation that has guided travelers for thousands of years.

The Great Bear

Ursa Major, meaning "Great Bear", is one of the most ancient and recognizable constellations in the night sky. Civilizations across the world have looked up at these stars and imagined stories written across the heavens.

For the Greeks it was a great celestial bear. For others it was a wagon, a plough, or a chariot moving slowly across the sky.

The Big Dipper

Within Ursa Major lies the famous star pattern known as the Big Dipper. Seven bright stars form a shape resembling a ladle.

For centuries sailors, travelers and explorers used these stars to navigate the night, because two of its stars always point toward Polaris, the North Star.

The Big Dipper Reveals Itself

A Symbol of Direction

The Big Dipper is more than just a pattern of stars. For generations it served as a compass in the darkness.

It reminds us that even in the deepest night, there are lights that quietly show the way.
