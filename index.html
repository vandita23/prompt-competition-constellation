URSA MAJOR

A constellation that helped lost travelers find direction in darkness.

The Great Bear

Ursa Major is one of the oldest constellations known to humanity. Ancient civilizations imagined it as a celestial bear watching over the northern sky.

Inside this large constellation lies a famous group of stars known as the Big Dipper. Seven stars forming a recognizable shape that has guided travelers for centuries.

The Hidden Compass

Two stars of the Big Dipper always point toward the North Star. Sailors and explorers once relied on this pattern when maps and technology did not exist.

Even in darkness, the sky quietly reminds us that direction is never truly lost.

The Big Dipper Appears

Never Lose Hope

Just as the Big Dipper points travelers toward the North Star, small moments of light in life can guide us through uncertainty.

Even the darkest sky is filled with stars waiting to show the way.
