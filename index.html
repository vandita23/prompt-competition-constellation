URSA MAJOR

Even the darkest sky contains stars that guide the lost.

The Great Bear

Ursa Major is one of the most recognizable constellations in the night sky. For thousands of years humans have looked toward these stars and imagined stories about the universe and our place within it.

Ancient cultures saw the constellation as a great cosmic bear walking through the heavens. Others saw a chariot, a wagon, or a hunter.

The Big Dipper

Inside Ursa Major lies one of the most famous star patterns in the sky — the Big Dipper. Seven bright stars form the shape of a large ladle.

Travelers used these stars for centuries to find direction in darkness. Two stars at the edge of the dipper always point toward Polaris, the North Star.

The Big Dipper Reveals Itself

A Symbol of Hope

For explorers, the Big Dipper was never just stars. It was direction, guidance, and hope.

Even when the night felt endless, these seven lights reminded people that the universe always leaves clues for those who look up.

Look Up

The sky above us has always carried stories of guidance and resilience. The stars remind us that darkness is never empty.

Sometimes all it takes is a few stars to show the way forward.
